Eames Office

About

For over 40 years, Eames Office was a wellspring of design innovation in multiple domains. Founded by husband-and-wife team Charles and Ray Eames, the output of the southern California space shaped American mid-century design and left its indelible stamp on post-war America.

Following two short stints in other locations (one of which was their apartment), the Eameses moved into 901 W. Washington Boulevard, Venice Beach, California. Dubbed “901”, the new space was organized to encourage exploration and invite curiosity. Through an iterative design process, the staff created iconic furniture, short films, public exhibitions, architecture, textiles, toys, and art that is appreciated to this day. Eames Office closed after the death of Ray Eames in 1988.

A partial list of notable contributions by Eames Office to the world of design:

  • The Eames House (also known as Case Study #8 for Arts & Architecture Digest) which is now a National Historic Landmark.
  • Furniture such as the Eames Lounge Chair and Ottoman (pictured above) and the LCW which Time magazine dubbed “the chair of the century”.
  • Eames Tandem Sling Seating which is used in hundreds of airports worldwide.
  • The IBM Pavilion at the New York World's Fair 1964-1965.
  • The award-winning short film Powers of Ten which is part of the United States National Film Registry.
